Reward expiry warnings
What we set out to build
Warn users about rewards expiring within 7 days, via push, behind one flag.
→
What we actually shipped
Warn users about rewards expiring within 14 days, via push and email, behind two flags. Per-reward dismiss added. Expired-section postponed.
8 divergences logged during the build. Three follow-up tickets spawned. One AC dropped, one AC added.
Divergences from spec
8 logged
Claude maintained this log during the build. Each entry is something that changed from the original spec, when it changed, and why.
01
Threshold extended: 7 days → 14 days
Day 2 · May 29
Sara (product) shared user research mid-sprint showing average engagement is bi-weekly, not weekly. A 7-day warning would miss users who only opened the app every 10 days. Extended to 14.
02
Email added back into scope
Day 3 · May 30
iOS push delivery was unreliable in QA — about 30% of test pushes failed silently. Email added as fallback channel so iOS users still got notified. Spawned CAS-1305 to investigate the underlying push issue. Note: email was explicitly a non-goal in the original spec.
03
Flag count: 1 → 2
Day 4 · May 31
Split the in-app banner and the notifications into separate flags for safer rollout. Banner rollout can be aggressive; notification rollout needs to be careful (you can't undo a sent push). New flags: reward-expiry-banner, reward-expiry-notifications.
04
AC dropped: Expired section postponed
Day 5 · Jun 01
Implementing the "Expired" section required broader changes to RewardsList layout than estimated. To hit ship date, postponed to CAS-1318. The core warning-before-expiry piece ships now.
05
AC added: Per-reward dismiss
Day 5 · Jun 01
Sara asked for it after seeing the staging build. "If a user knows they're going to use it, the warning becomes noise." Added a dismiss button with per-reward state persistence.
06
Open question resolved: Banner component
Day 1 · May 28
Design system already had a <WarningBanner> component. Reused. Saved roughly half a day.
07
Open question partial: Timezone
Day 4 · May 31
Shipped using UTC for cron evaluation, with a one-line README note that this is a known approximation. Spawned CAS-1312 for proper local-timezone handling. Acceptable for v1 because the 14-day threshold makes boundary errors low-impact.
08
Open question resolved: Batching
Day 3 · May 30
Shipped with one daily digest push per user, listing all rewards expiring in the next 14 days. Decision made in PR review — multiple pushes felt spammy.
